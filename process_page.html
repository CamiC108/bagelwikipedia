THINKING

Picture Selection

a. First of all, to save time, I went through my albums to see if there is any picture I can use in this site.

b. Then, I found that I have a lot of pictures about bagel. They were taken during my internship at a Chinese bakery. My colleague and I collaborated on these photos. They are taken by Sony a7m3. I decided to use these photos.

Topic Decision

a. Since most of the pictures I chosen are about Bagel, so it is easier for me to choose Bagel as the topic.

b. To verify whether the Wikipedia site of Bagel meet the requirement or not, I had checked the Wikipedia. It has 8 subsections of content that fulfills the requirement.

c. I made the topic Bagel. The Wikipedia site of Bagel is the content base.

Model Website Selection

a. My requirements for the model website are that the complexity of the design must be at my level and that it must be styled to fit the theme of Bagel.

b. I chose uwaterloo.ca.

PLANNING

Content Filtering

Since Wikipedia has too much content, it is not quite right to put it all on my site, I choose six of the most important section to be my structural navigation and main content in the landing page. I put the rest of them to the footer as utility navigation so that users can access them.

Landing Page & Inner Page

The home page of my website mimics the University of Waterloo's official landing page, and my “About Bagel” page mimics the “About Waterloo” page on the University of Waterloo website.

Information Architecture

Before formalizing the site, I drew out the Information architecture of the entire site, using the tool Lucidchart.

EXECUTION

Logo Design

The logo of my site is designed by Adobe Illustrator. I designed an animated likeness of a bagel based on its shape for my logo.
